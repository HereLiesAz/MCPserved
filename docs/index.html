AUTHORIZED

The idea

Authority comes from the device. Never the network.

The phone decides — nothing else. A model, a script, an assistant on your laptop: each gets in only through a connection you opened, and touches only what you allowed.

Local

It never phones home

Control rides a USB cable or an adb-over-Wi-Fi session you started. No internet. No server. Your screen stays between the two machines.

Authorized

Dead until you say go

An empty grant table can't touch a thing. No denylist to keep current — only what you switched on.

Transparent

You watch it work

Every session is timed, pinned to an ongoing notification with a stop button, and logged action by action.

Revocable

Two ways to kill it

Empty the grants and it can't act. Rotate the key and it can't connect. Uninstall and it's gone.

adb

Two backends, one interface

Start with adb. Upgrade to the app.

Same tools either way — the model can't tell them apart. The app just gives the phone more to say back.

Default · quick connect

adb

No app. Any phone with USB debugging or adb-over-Wi-Fi.

  • Gestures & keys — input
  • UI tree — uiautomator dump
  • Screenshots — screencap
  • A full shell, device-wide — exactly what USB debugging grants
no per-app grants · no clipboard · no notification list
Upgrade · paired app

on-device app

Tunnels to the app's loopback server over an adb forward it opens itself.

  • Accessibility backend — semantic tree, real text entry
  • Per-package grants, scoped and expiring
  • Timed sessions with an ongoing notification
  • Clipboard and notification mirror
sealed frames · loopback only · falls back to adb

X25519

Security model

Local. And still sealed.

Loopback isn't a wall — any app on the phone can dial a local port. The pairing key decides who gets an answer.

Pairing

X25519, out of band

Both public keys go by QR, both directions. Nothing sits in the handshake. Keys stay encrypted on the device and never leave it.

Frames

ChaCha20-Poly1305

Every message is sealed and authenticated. A frame that won't open gets no reply — not even a hint the device is there.

Per connection

Fresh keys every time

A random salt in the hello derives new keys per connection. Relaunch the client; no nonce ever repeats.

Reach

127.0.0.1, full stop

The channel binds to IPv4 loopback and dials nothing out. Reachable only through an adb-forward tunnel you set up.

ARM

Setup

Plugged in to acting, in four.

1

Reach it with adb

USB debugging on and plugged in — or adb connect <ip>:5555 over the network. Check with adb devices.

2

Point your host at the server

Run mcpserved. Unpaired, it drives the phone straight over adb. Ready now.

3

Optional: pair the app

Want accessibility, grants, and the notification mirror? Install the app, run mcpserved pair, and scan both ways.

4

Arm, grant, go

Accept the disclosure, enable the service, arm it, and grant the packages you want reachable. The server upgrades to the app on its own.

CAVEATS

Honest constraints

What it won't fake.

The screen stays on in an app session

Lock the device and the node tree empties. Keep sessions short.

Shizuku dies on reboot

No root means a manual re-pair after every restart.

adb text is ASCII-ish

Spaces work; newlines and most non-ASCII don't. The app handles the rest.

adb has no clipboard or notifications

Those need the paired app.

iOS is out

No third-party iOS app can automate another. Apple's rule, not ours.

Play still wants disclosure

The relay's gone, but accessibility use is declared — with a prominent in-app notice.
